Falcon 9

The Falcon 9 is a two-stage rocket developed and manufactured by SpaceX. It is the company's workhorse rocket and is designed for both satellite launches and cargo resupply missions to the International Space Station (ISS). The first stage of the Falcon 9 is powered by nine Merlin engines, while the second stage uses one Merlin Vacuum engine. Both stages use a combination of liquid oxygen (LOX) and rocket-grade kerosene (RP-1) propellants. The first stage is reusable and can land either on a drone ship in the ocean or on land, allowing for cost savings and multiple launches. The Falcon 9 has been successfully launching and deploying various satellites into orbit since its maiden launch in 2010. It has also been utilized by NASA for cargo resupply missions to the ISS as part of the Commercial Resupply Services (CRS) program. In addition to these missions, SpaceX has used the Falcon 9 to launch the company's own Starlink satellites for building a global broadband internet constellation. The Falcon 9 also serves as the platform for launching the Crew Dragon spacecraft with astronauts on board for NASA's Commercial Crew Program. Overall, the Falcon 9 has significantly impacted the space industry by demonstrating reusable rocket technology and enabling more affordable and frequent access to space.

Falcon Heavy


The Falcon 9 Heavy is a partially reusable heavy-lift launch vehicle developed by SpaceX. It is an upgraded version of the Falcon 9 rocket and is currently the most powerful operational rocket in the world. The Falcon 9 Heavy consists of three Falcon 9 first stages strapped together, forming a total of 27 Merlin engines that produce over 5 million pounds of thrust at liftoff. This allows it to carry large payloads to orbit, including satellites and potentially even crewed missions to the Moon or Mars in the future. The most notable achievement of the Falcon 9 Heavy was its successful launch of the Falcon Heavy Test Flight in February 2018, where it became the first operational rocket to have such a high payload capacity.
